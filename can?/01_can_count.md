📖 When referring to **cardinal numbers** (how many), such as **one**, **two**, and **three**, we use the basic number forms.

📖 When referring to **ordinal numbers** (position or order), we use **first**, **second**, **third**, etc.

Exp:

- **Cardinal**:  
  - I have **two** apples.  
  - There are **five** books on the table.
  
- **Ordinal**:  
  - This is my **first** lesson.  
  - He came in **second** place in the race.

Ordinal Numbers Examples:

- **First** – first
- **Second** – second
- **Third** – third
- **Fourth** – fourth
- **Fifth** – fifth
- **Sixth** – sixth
- **Eleventh** – eleventh
- **Twentieth** – twentieth
- **Thirtieth** – thirtieth
- **Hundredth** – hundredth

📖 When I want say **5 - 3 * 20** I say - **Five minus three times twenty**

📖 When I want say **9 / 12 * (3 + 2)** I say - **Nine divided by twelve times the sum of three and two**

📖 When I want say **9^12** I say - **Nine to the power of twelve**

📖 When I want to say **√25**, I say - **The square root of twenty-five.**

**+** - plus

**-** - minus

**/** - divided

**_*_** - times

**()** - parentheses

**=** – equals

**√** – square root  

**^** – to the power of (exponentiation)
